Gas-Based Fire Protection Systems

Effective and clean fire suppression using inert and chemical gases, ideal for protecting sensitive environments and valuable assets.

Overview of Gas-Based Fire Protection Systems

For environments that demand clean and efficient fire suppression, our gas-based systems are the perfect solution. Using inert or chemical gases, these systems quickly suppress fires without leaving any residue, making them ideal for protecting sensitive environments such as data centers, laboratories, and archival storage.

How Gas-Based Fire Protection Systems Work?

Gas-based fire suppression systems operate by releasing inert or chemical gases into the protected area when a fire is detected. These gases either displace the oxygen in the environment or interrupt the chemical reactions necessary for combustion, effectively extinguishing the fire. The key advantage of these systems is their ability to suppress fires without causing damage to sensitive equipment or leaving any residue that would require cleanup.

Types of Gases Used

  • Inert Gases

    These include gases like nitrogen, argon, and carbon dioxide. Inert gases work by reducing the oxygen concentration in the protected area to a level that will not support combustion. They are safe for use in occupied spaces and do not produce harmful by-products.

  • Chemical Gases

    These include agents like FM-200 (HFC-227ea), Novec 1230, and Halon alternatives. Chemical gases suppress fires by absorbing heat or by interrupting the chemical reaction of the fire. These agents are highly effective and leave no residue, making them ideal for environments with delicate equipment.

Key Advantages of Gas-Based Fire Protection Systems

  • Key Advantages of Gas-Based Fire Protection Systems

    One of the primary benefits of gas-based systems is their clean suppression method. Unlike water-based systems, gas-based fire suppression does not leave behind any residue, making it perfect for environments where cleanliness is paramount.

  • Rapid Response

    Gas-based systems are designed for quick detection and suppression, ensuring that fires are dealt with promptly before they can cause significant damage. This rapid response is crucial in environments where even a small fire can have devastating consequences.

  • Minimal Downtime

    By avoiding water damage and residue cleanup, gas-based systems help ensure minimal operational downtime. This is especially important in critical facilities where continuity is essential.

  • Safe for Equipment

    Gas-based fire suppression is gentle on sensitive equipment, making it ideal for use in data centers, server rooms, laboratories, and other high-tech environments where traditional fire suppression methods might cause damage.
